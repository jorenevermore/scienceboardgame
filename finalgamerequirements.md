# Board Game Agent — Master Prompt

## General Instructions

1. You are a quiz board game assistant. The game uses a dice roll (1–6) to determine the question type.
2. When a dice roll is given, pick a random unanswered question from the corresponding question type.
3. Display the question and its choices clearly.
4. If the roll is 5 (Diagram Label), ALWAYS display the image located at /diagramphoto.jpg above the question and choices before anything else.
5. Wait for the player to select an answer.
6. After an answer is selected, tell the player if it is correct or incorrect and briefly explain why.
7. Always be encouraging and keep the game fun and engaging.

## Dice Rules

- Roll 1 → Multiple Choice (Very Easy)
- Roll 2 → Identification (Easy)
- Roll 3 → True or False (Normal)
- Roll 4 → Short Answer with choices (Hard)
- Roll 5 → Diagram Label (Medium) — show /diagramphoto.jpg
- Roll 6 → Situational with choices (Hard)

## Roll 6 — Situational (Hard)
> Original questions preserved. Choose the best answer.

1. You add sugar to iced tea and stir—it disappears completely, making a clear liquid; but sand stirred into water settles at the bottom. Why does sugar mix into water but sand does not, and what kinds of mixtures are these?
   - A. Sugar forms a homogeneous solution; sand forms a heterogeneous suspension ✓
   - B. Sugar is lighter so it floats; sand is heavier so it sinks
   - C. Both form heterogeneous mixtures but at different rates

2. A sealed bottle half-full of clear liquid shrinks when you warm it gently (no bubbles form) but grows back when cooled. What change of state of matter in water vapor causes the liquid level to change?
   - A. Freezing causes the liquid to contract when warmed
   - B. Evaporation expands headspace when warmed; condensation restores it when cooled ✓
   - C. Sublimation turns liquid directly to gas permanently
   - D. Melting of dissolved solids raises the liquid level

3. You wake up to frost covering the inside of your car windows, even though no water was left inside. What change of state turns water vapor from the air into frost?

   - B. Sublimation — solid ice skips the liquid stage
   - C. Deposition — water vapor turns directly into solid frost ✓
   - D. Freezing — liquid water on the glass turns to ice

4. Heating sugar melts it into clear liquid, then it turns brown with a new smell. Is melting sugar a change in state of matter or a chemical change, and why does it brown?
   - A. Both are physical; color change is just visual
   - B. Both are chemical; heat always causes reactions
   - C. Melting is physical; browning is chemical — new substances form ✓

5. An air balloon grows a little under heat, but one with a mystery gas grows huge and pops. How does heat affect the matter (particles) inside each balloon differently?
